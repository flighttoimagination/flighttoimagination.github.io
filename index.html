McKenzie Paddock

Pointillist | Printmaker

I’m a transplant from New England, making my artist debut in Minnesota. The creative souls and community I’ve found during my cherished years here in the Twin Cities have made this place feel like home. Through my art, I hope to give back to the community by helping people feel seen or offering the little dopamine boosts we’re all desperately needing these days. I’m passionate about process over product, working primarily with printmaking and pointillism. Each piece can take me countless hours of catharsis, frustration, experimentation, delayed gratification, existential crisis, and flow. What you see in each creation is only a sliver of the story so feel free to ask me about the rest or fill it in with your own lived experience. I’m here to bring flight to imagination.

Process Over Product

I was first absorbed into process-oriented thinking while studying art + design with a concentration in printmaking at Salem State University. During my studies, I had the privilege of witnessing professors demonstrate art making processes in real time. Each step by step instruction offered guidance for an expansive way of utilizing art media. I fell in love with discoveries made in the space between the steps. My fascination with the art process as a metaphor for life is also highly influenced by Georges Seurat and Wassily Kandinsky as well as formal training in art as therapy. My creations do not represent the successful completion of product but rather an evolution of visual responses to feelings and imagined ideas.

Pointillism

Fine tip pens are a treasured instrument for my reality transforming need to create. Through different sizes and densities of black ink dots, I experiment with texture and form. With a special interest in color theory, I’ve taken my exploration of composition even further. My pointillism obsession started over a decade ago and has offered me many lessons, one of which being that there is always space for more dots. This means that each piece will forever be a process. I’m often fooled into thinking a piece is complete until I revisit it years later to find I desire adding layers to make it grow, just as I have.

Printmaking

When I have the capacity in my physical and mental space, I return to my beloved printmaking. I’m compelled by the creative problem solving and delayed gratification inherent in the printmaking process. You’ll find most of my works use the relief printing process through carved wood or linoleum blocks. I’m also tickled by the opposite techniques of intaglio in which I incise metal plates with the image I want to print rather than carving the negative space. Refer to my process images to learn more.
